DRD-05.03 - Certificate of ISS Payload Safety Compliance (JSC Form 1114)
Reference: JSC Form 1114A Certificate of NSTS/ISS Payload Safety Compliance
NSTS/ISS 13830 Payload Safety Review and Data Submittal Requirements
Purpose: The purpose of this document/form is to document the compliance with the NSTS/ISS payload safety requirements (for GSE design and ground operations). Required verification data: Certificate of Compliance (COC) with the requirement. The objective of the safety review process is to review the payload, GSE, and operations for adequate safety implementation. The mission success and any scientific objectives of the payload are the responsibility of the PO and are beyond the scope of this document and process.
Content: All projects shall certify that the flight and ground system products are safe and in compliance with the requirements of safety relevant standards as well as any applicable project specific safety requirements. The certification process shall be completed prior to delivery to the customer. The PO must present a signed certificate of the flight payload safety compliance to the PSRP executive secretary no later than 10 days prior to the Flight Readiness Review (FRR). The phase III SDP shall contain a signed Certificate of Safety Compliance. The safety analysis results shall be documented in the SDP, which includes applicable payload HR forms (JSC Form 542B and JSC Form 1230). Guidelines for completing the flight HR forms and preparing the SDP are found in JSC 26943. Each ISS HR should stand alone. Supporting data (e.g. certifications) shall be prepared where applicable and also submitted for review (phase III HRs).
Format:
  • JSC Form 1114A.
  • The PSRP will accept "equivalent" forms (e.g. those that contain all the required information) developed by the PO.
