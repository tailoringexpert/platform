DRD-05.09 - ESA: Safety Data Package (SDP)
Reference: ECSS-Q-ST-40C Space product assurance - Safety
ECSS-M-ST-80C Space Project Management - Risk Management
Purpose: The purpose of the SDP is to document the compliance with the project relevant safety requirements. Defines the payload activities and documentation requirements leading to safety documentation of instruments, facilities, mission equipment and instrument ground support equipment (IGSE) to support the payload safety review process. The supplier is required to conduct a systematic safety analysis and to document and submit a SDP in support of safety reviews to be conducted by the operators. The customer shall perform the safety reviews which document the current safety status of the system. The SDP shall be the subject of review and formal acceptance by the customer and the launch authority. The safety critical items shall be identified and listed. Deviations from safety requirements shall be identified. All safety critical changes made to technical requirements shall be approved and agreed.
Content: This ESA standard is applicable to all European space projects where during any project phase there exist the potential for hazards. The supplier shall prepare and submit the SDP to the safety reviews. Safety reviews shall be performed at all phases (A, B, C, D, E) necessary to ensure satisfactory implementation of safety requirements. A SDP shall be prepared for each review. This may be a stand-alone package or may be integrated into the overall data package if the safety review is part of an overall project review. All safety critical items and safety critical functions shall be qualified.The typical content of the SDP is indicated for each milestone review in following table.
Format The following data are required for the simplified SDP for basic payloads:
  • description of the HW design and flight operations;
  • safety risk assessment (if applicable);
  • summary of the safety analysis results (Safety analysis report);
  • documentation of all applicable hazards, controls and verifications on HR;
  • supporting analysis (if applicable);
  • hazardous ground operations list and procedures;
  • safety verification tracking log (SVTL).
