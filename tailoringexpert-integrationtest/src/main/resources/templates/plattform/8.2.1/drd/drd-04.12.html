DRD-04.12 - Worst Case Analysis (WCA)
Reference: ECSS-Q-HB-30-01A Space product assurance - Worst Case Analysis
ECSS-Q-ST-30-11 C Rev.1 Space Product Assurance - Derating - EEE Components
ECSS-Q-TM-30-12A Space product assurance -  End-of-life Parameter Drifts - EEE Components
Purpose: To demonstrate the capability and margins in the design of electronic and electrical circuits and electromechanical items to operate satisfactorily during the whole of specified mission taking into account worst case conditions of manufacturing tolerances, parameter drift and deviations due to temperature, ionizing radiation and ageing.
Content: Worst-case Analyses (WCA) shall be accomplished at equipment level. The analysis has to be performed on SPF's and hazardous items with special emphasis on analogue circuitry including power supplies, RF-circuitry, critical interfaces e.g. ECL devices and optocouplers and CMOS as well as power-MOS devices which are susceptible to ionizing radiation. The parameter degradation given in ECSS-Q-ST-30-11 and ECSS-Q-TM-30-12 shall be applied. Any adaptation of these data to the actual mission duration or the use of other data sources is subject for approval by DLR. Radiation dose values shall be taken from separate radiation analysis which takes into account the internal radiation shielding of the analysed equipment. In case of use of radiation non-resistant components, data on parameter degradation caused by radiation shall be provided by representative sample tests. At least twice the maximum degradation resulting from these tests shall be considered for worst case analysis. Non-compliances with the specified functional requirements which cannot be settled are subject to a RFW.
Format: The parameter deviation data shall be presented in tabular form. The in-house format containing sufficient information on the assumptions, method and results shall be used.
