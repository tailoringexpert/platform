DRD-05.08 - NASA: Safety Data Package (SDP)
Reference: SSP-51700 Baseline Payload Safety Policy and Requirements for the International Space Station
NSTS/ISS 13830 Payload Safety Review and Data Submittal Requirements
Purpose: The purpose of the SDP is to document the compliance with the project relevant safety requirements. Defines the payload activities and documentation requirements leading to safety documentation of instruments, facilities, mission equipment and instrument ground support equipment (IGSE) to support the payload safety review process. The PO is required to conduct a systematic safety analysis and to document and submit a SDP in support of safety reviews to be conducted by the operators. The customer shall perform the safety reviews which document the current safety status of the system. The SDP shall be the subject of review and formal acceptance by the customer and the launch authority. The safety critical items shall be identified and listed. Deviations from safety requirements shall be identified. All safety critical changes made to technical requirements shall be approved and agreed.
Content: The safety analysis results shall be documented in the SDP, which includes applicable payload HR forms (JSC Form 542B and JSC Form 1230). The customer will perform phase (0, I, II, III) safety reviews. The supplier shall submit the SDP to the Payload Mission Manager (PMM). And the PMM provides the SDP to the Safety Panel for the phase review (PSRP, GSRP). The SDP shall describe the manufactured flight and ground HW/SW and its operations and shall define the potential hazards and the implemented hazard control methods (as applicable). Every data or documentation changes in the SDP resulting by mandatory modifications will be formally controlled. The supplier shall integrate safety data related to the various subsystems or equipment that make up the system into the SDP that is to be presented at the safety reviews. The data shall be updated as necessary for each of the phased reviews. At the phase III review, the data should show that the verification process has been completed. Safety Compliance Data Package: Special data submittal requirements have been developed for payloads using standards elements to eliminate unnecessary duplication of activities (e.g. safety activities).
Format The following data are required for the simplified SDP for basic payloads:
  • description of the HW design and flight operations;
  • summary of the safety analysis results;
  • documentation of all applicable hazards, controls and verifications on HR (e.g. JSC Form 1230 / Form 542B);
  • Payload Flight Safety VTL (JSC Form 764);
  • certificate of Payload Safety Compliance (e.g. JSC Form 1114A).
