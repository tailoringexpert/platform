DRD-03.12 - Operational and Technical Documentation (for Projects with RSC Energia)
Reference: SP Korolev Rocket and Space Corporation Energie International Space Station Russian Segment - General Technical Requirements for Payload
SSP 50094 NASA/RSA Specifications Standards Document for the ISS Russian Segment
SSP 50146 NASA/RSA Bilateral S&MA Process Requirements for International Space Station
SSP 41163 Russian Segment Specification
Purpose: The supplier shall establish a formal acceptance for all deliverable items, at any contractual level, to ensure that conformance of the items to be delivered is fully assess and documented. The operational and technical documentation, which requirements are addressed below will be developed for each HW unit (HW names) to be uploaded and operated on the ISS RS modules.
Content: The operational and technical documentation is the collection of the data related to the assembly, integration and test of a deliverable configuration item which provides the necessary traceability and events records. The operational and technical documentation is to provide all necessary information about:
  • qualification, concerning traceability, tests,
  • as-built configuration and data for operation by the customer, and
  • safety assurance.
The operational and technical documentation required for the Acceptance Test (AT) shall reflect the latest as-designed status for the delivered HW. The complete set of QT and AT documentation (list of documents is to be agreed by both sides) shall be submitted to the Russian side for agreement not later than one month before the beginning of AT-1 (at the supplier's premises) in accordance with the agreed activities schedule. A updated set of documentation based on QT and QT-1 results shall be presented at AT-2 (at Russian premises).
The documentation is prepared both in the Russian and English languages. Test sheets for QT presented in No. 206 can be presented in English with no translation.
A copy of the data package shall be submitted to customer at least TBD days prior to the delivery review.
Format: Structure use following list, the completeness is to be checked against the list. Any exceptions from this rule are additional agreed by the partners.
Doc No Title Content
100 Technical Specification for the Space Experiment (TS-EX) The Technical Specification (TS) for the Space Experiment (SE) is a basic document, which defines the scientific and technical rationale/justification of the SE objective, all requirements for the programme and methodical, technical support of SE, resources, and commitments of the SE participants.
TS for the SE shall consists of the following sections:
  1. Introduction, which describes SE in general
  2. SE Objectives
  3. HW Requirements
  4. Requirements for SE Sessions Support Equipment
  5. Requirements for SE Ground Preparation and Verification
  6. Technical Requirements for the ISS RS for SE Performance
  7. Commitments of SE Participants
200 Technical Specification for the Hardware (TS-EQ) The Technical Specification (TS) for the Equipment (EQ) is a basic document, which identifies all technical HW requirements according to which the HW will be accepted after the development and a cycle of qualification tests.
The TS for the EQ shall consists of the following sections
  1. Introduction, which defines the purpose of HW development
  2. HW Complement
  3. Technical Requirements. Among these are:
    • Functional requirements
    • Requirements for electric and
    • electromagnetic compatibility
    • Requirements for resistance to environmental factors
    • Requirements for resistance to mechanical effects
    • Characteristics of HW (vehicle)-produced acoustic noises
    • Safety requirements
    • Reliability requirements
    • Requirements for SS externally installed HW
    • Labeling requirements
    • Packing requirements
    • Structural requirements / mechanical parameters
    • Requirements for maintenance, repair and storage convenience
    • Transport requirements
  4. SW and Information Support Requirements (if needed)
  5. Requirements for Training (Education/Training) Models and Dedicated Mockups
  6. Requirements for Ground Technological Equipment (Control and Test Equipment)
  7. Requirements for Technical Documentation
  8. Requirements for Protection of Author's Rights
  9. Development Phases
  10. Test and Acceptance Test Procedure
201 Technical Description (TD) The Technical Description (TD) is intended to study the HW (instrument) and shall include the HW description, as well as technical data which are required for HW operation.
The technical description shall consists of the following main sectors:
  1. Introduction
  2. Purpose
  3. HW Complement
  4. Technical Data (performances)
  5. HW Design (structure)
  6. HW Components Design (structure)
  7. Data Interface Characteristics
  8. HW Operation Description
  9. Test Instruments (for incoming inspection)
  10. Tolls and Accessories, Consumables
  11. Labeling and Sealing
  12. Packing and Container
  13. Appendices (if needed)
202 Operation and Maintenance Manual (OM) The Operations and Maintenance Manual (OM) documents the data required for correct operation of the HW (instrument), handling and maintenance rules, rules for activities step-by-step performance (including both ground and onboard operations in flight operation), needed tools, etc.
The Operation Manual (OM) shall consists of the following sections:
  1. Introduction (general instructions)
  2. Safety Measures (instruction of safety measures)
  3. Preparation for Operation
  4. Pre-operation Check of Technical Status
  5. Operation Procedure
  6. Maintenance
  7. Possible Malfunctions and Trouble-Shooting
  8. Storage Rules
  9. Transportation
  10. Appendices.
203 Acceptance Test Program/Procedure The document specifies the scope and procedure of the Acceptance Test (AT) at the HW (equipment) designed for installation and operation aboard the ISS RS module.
The AT Program / Procedure shall consists of the following sections:
  1. Introduction
  2. Test Object and Purpose
  3. General Provisions
  4. Test Conditions and Sequence
  5. Control and Test Equipment
  6. Scope of AT
  7. Test Performance Procedure including the Subsections on
    • Completeness check
    • Visual examination
    • Dimensional check
    • Mass and Center of Gravity (CoG) check
    • Electrical resistance and insulation breakdown check
    • Amplitude and inrush current check
    • Verification of power consumption during nominal operation
    • Faultless operation verification
    • Inspection of Packing
    • Vibration test during launch and injection
    • Safety check
  8. Appendices including
    • Flight model functional check procedure
    • Complement and AT procedure for training models
    • AT certificate.
204 Incoming Inspection and Functional Check Instruction (Program/Procedure) The document defines the incoming inspection operations related to flight HW models after transportation and before launch aboard the vehicle.
The document shall consists of the following basic sections:
  1. Introduction
  2. Preparation for Activities, including
    • Requirements for the test rooms
    • Constraints of environmental parameters
  3. General Provisions
  4. Ground Support Equipment
  5. Visual Inspection Procedure
  6. HW Functional Verification
  7. Prelaunch Readiness Check
205 Qualification Test Instruction (Program/Procedure) The document regulates the Qualification Test (QT) sequence, scope and procedure.
The document shall consists of the following sections:
  1. Introduction
  2. Test Object
  3. Test Conditions and Sequence
  4. Qualification Test Scope and Procedure
  5. Appendices covering the HW functional check procedure following the QT.
206 QT Reports The document is composed of the report (reports) on the HW (equipment) qualification tests performed at the developer.
The document shall consists of the following sections:
  1. Introduction
  2. General Provisions
  3. Test Conditions and Sequence
  4. Qualification Test Scope
  5. Qualification Test Procedure and resulting including
    • Vibration acceleration tests during ground transportation
    • Vibration acceleration tests in launch phase
    • Vibration acceleration tests in descent and landing phase
    • On-orbit vibration acceleration tests
    • Shock loads tests in launch phase
    • Shock loads tests in descent and landing phase
    • Linear acceleration tests in launch phase
    • Linear acceleration tests in descent and landing phase
    • Temperature test during transportation
    • 95% relative humidity tests
    • Thermal cycle tests
    • Increased oxygen (40%) environment tests
    • Increased (970 mm Hg) or decreased (450 mm Hg) pressures tests
    • Vacuum tests with solar simulation
    • Electromagnetic compatibility tests
    • HW-produced acoustic noise tests
    • Radiation tests
    • Safety verifications
    • Reliability verifications
    • Leak verifications
  6. Appendix with tests results.
207 Safety Reports and Certificates Generally the document has no strictly defined form, however, it shall contain all necessary data which will allow to make a well-defined conclusion about the safety of the HW to be launched on space transport vehicles and used on the ISS RS modules both for the station crew members and the station itself in all HW utilization (operation) phases. A list of the HW safety requirements is given in the technical requirements for the interfaces and HW to be installed on the ISS RS modules.
Based on the safety requirements as identified in the technical requirements for interfaces and HW to be installed on the ISS RS modules the Safety Report and Certificates shall include the following sections:
  1. Introduction containing subsection:
    • Purpose of document
    • purpose and complement of HW
  2. HW Description (unit-by-unit)
  3. Materials Safety containing subsections:
    • Fire safety
    • Toxicological safety (Off-gassing)
    • Microbiological safety
    • Material aging
  4. Electrical Safety including subsections:
    • Insulation
    • High voltage
    • Electromagnetic interference
  5. Structure Safety including subsections:
    • Protection against small particles
    • External design configuration
    • Controls
    • Electromagnetic and other radiation
    • Gas bottles handling system (if needed)
    • Noise level
    • Temperature
  6. Appendices (mandatory) containing:
    • Safety verification results
    • Toxicological tests data
    • List of non-metallic materials
    • Materials conclusion
208 Input Data for Crew Onboard Procedures The document regulates the crew training procedure to handle the HW when performing the experiment aboard the ISS RS module. The document shall cover only that information which is needed for crew training. In addition, it shall include the requirement for handling the training equipment and making up lessons schedule. The document shall take into consideration the fact that the crew training is aimed at successful conduct of a definite scientific experiment as indicated in TS-EQ by using a definite quantity of instruments (equipment). Since several units of equipment can be used in one experiment, this document is made up such that the crew training covers training and handling all systems, which will be used in the experiment.
The document shall consists of the following sections:
  1. Introduction
  2. Description of Scientific Research Programme
  3. Flight HW Complement (Description)
  4. Flight Operation including subsection:
    • Equipment transfer constraints
    • Operations
    • Operations procedure in off-nominal situations
  5. Training HW Complement (description) including a subsection:
    • Operations procedure for training equipment
  6. Training and Flight HW Safety Requirements
  7. Description of Training (Studies)
209 Technical Description of Ground Test Equipment The document gives a list and description of the Ground Test Equipment/Control Test Equipment (GTE/CTE) designed for performing all types of tests and checks of a specific HW (equipment) in ground conditions.
The document shall consists of the following sections:
  1. Introduction
  2. List of Ground Test Equipment
  3. Description of Ground Test Equipment

210 Electrical Circuit Diagrams The electrical circuit diagrams shall contain data on electrical cable connections between separate HW units, as well as between the HW and electrical power supply system, onboard computer system, telemetry of specific ISS RS Module.
The diagrams shall include:
  1. Cable network characteristics including physical characteristics and length of the cables, type and cross-section of wires, shieldings requirements, manufacturer, and other necessary information
  2. Description of the electrical connections of separate HW units and data on specific connection on the station HW (including the indication of methods to ground the electrical network components on the HW housing and the station module hull)
  3. Data related to the electrical connectors being used (type, pin assignment).
211 Outline-Installation Drawing (OID) The outline-installation drawings (OID) are signed by the Russian side and the partner and specify the HW location (installation) features on the vehicle during the launch and on-orbit flight.
The OID presents the following information:
  1. HW mass with tolerances
  2. Coordination of center of mass with tolerances
  3. Initial moments of inertia
  4. Preferred orientation during launch or statement about an inapplicability of the requirements
  5. Coordinates of locations of electrical connectors on the HW housing
  6. Pin assignment and making of cable connectors
  7. Reference to documentation containing a description of HW basic characteristics and operational manual
  8. HW name and labels location
  9. Indication of all HW elements, which shall be deinstalled (connectors covers, transport fasteners, etc.) Their labels, storage location, as well as deinstallation method and time shall be indicated
  10. Drawings explanations reflecting technical requirements for installation
  11. Indication of free zone required for HW access, and their components, for example, service ports and hatches, envelop areas, etc.
  12. Limitation of cable length (for example, distance from SM interface to the PL system controller shall not be more than 3 meters)
  13. Indication of all transport equipment, removable handles and free zones required for operations with the indication of dimension.
  14. HW grounding elements and specific grounding methods to be identified in the drawing comments
  15. All protective covers (for keyboards, buttons, front panels, electrical connectors, etc.)
  16. Temperature requirements and description of technical characteristics of the HW (for example, size and location of inlets and outlets of the air cooling system, outlet air temperature, and air flow rate based on the specified inlet air temperature, direction of inlet/outlet air flow and free zones for access to inlet/outlet fans
  17. HW configuration for various phases of its preparation and utilization (for example, transportation, launch, utilization, storage)
  18. HW fastening methods with the indication of sizes and materials, quantity of attachment points, location and size of holes, thickness of materials, type of coating (anodized, chemical coating) or other specific requirements (excluding references to military standards, primary chemical components and impurities percentage shall be specified)
  19. Indication of all types of HW components (with dimensions) which are required for adequate definition of all HW components (drawings scales shall be presented such that the dimensions, which are not indicated in the drawing, can be measured and/or calculated).
