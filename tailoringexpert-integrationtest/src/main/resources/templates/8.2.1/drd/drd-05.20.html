Purpose: Fire protection requirements reflect the operating environment that is of extended duration with low personnel density in the habitable volumes. These requirements are intended to be consistent with the standard operational ISS crew responses where practical using standardized design and operations approaches. Fire event detection is required to provide timely notification to the crew of a fire event for all potential fire event locations within the payload. Spacecraft fire-safety strategy emphasizes prevention, mostly through the selection of onboard items classified according to their fire resistance. For human-crew spacecraft, fire prevention is promoted by the use of materials and assemblies that are classified and selected by their fire resistance Experience has shown that combustible materials will accumulate (i.e., waste products, testing equipment materials, documents, etc.), configurations will change, and the chance of human error will increase.

JSC Form 1428 is applicable to all ISS Payloads. Instructions for the completion of Fire Detection and Suppression Reporting Form follow:
  1. Number
    A unique alphanumeric designation provided by the payload organization or hardware developer that will be used to track this hazard report. The prefix "FDS" is used to identify this report as a Fire Detection and Suppression Report.
  2. Mission Phase
    Identify applicable mission phase e.g. Increment # tbd, On-Orbit Operations.
  3. Payload Name
    Full name of payload with abbreviated name.
  4. Fire Prevention
    Cross reference JSC Form 1230 and applicable hazards e.g. "Electrical Power Distribution" and "Flammability".
  5. Payload Volume
    Unique volumes to be identified with name, e.g. Rack, Container, Box, etc.
  6. Fire Detection Method
    Identified method used to assure the effectiveness of the hazard control.
  7. Verification Method, Reference and Status
    Summarize the results of the analyses, or completed tests. Refer to particular test reports by document number and title. Indicate the status of the activity.
  8. Suppression Method
    Identified method used to assure the effectiveness of the hazard control.
  9. Verification Method, Reference and Status
    Summarize the results of the analyses, or completed tests. Refer to particular test reports by document number and title. Indicate the status of the activity.
