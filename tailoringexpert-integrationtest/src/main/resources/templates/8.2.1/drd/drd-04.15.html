DRD-04.15 - Maintenance Plan (MP)
Reference: SD-PL-AI-0092 Safety Centered Maintainability for ISS Payloads - Maintenance Planning Guideline for ISS P/Ls
GPQ-MAN-04 Safety Centered Maintainability for ISS Payloads - Maintainability Handbook
ECSS-M-ST-10C Space project management - Project planning and implementation
ECSS-M-70A Space project management - Integrated Logistic Support
Purpose: The Maintenance Plan (MP) shall support the maintenance concept adopted to satisfy operational requirements. Maintenance planning is the process that develops the anticipated maintenance requirements for the subsystems/equipment and proposes who will do required preventive maintenance tasks and where they will be done. The tasks shall be applicable and effective to ISS P/Ls support in accordance with ISS requirements. MP defines what is to do during the life cycle of an ISS P/L.
The use of P/Ls is facing two basic needs:
  • Safe operations: Payloads shall not endanger the life of the ISS crew or the ISS itself;
  • Low cost: perform scientific research to minimum cost, e.g. minimize the experiment running costs.
Content: MP is an engineering process to identify maintenance needs to support the system availability during all project phases. As such it requires to be defined and structured in order to be well harmonized into the higher levels design process. A part of the process is the identification of HW that need maintenance and definition of which type maintenance is required.
As a minimum, the following shall be identified and described in a MP:
  • training programmes, facilities and equipment;
  • technical data necessary to enable personnel to operate and maintain the equipment;
  • special aids depicting tool usage, failure isolation, repair/replacement procedures and other maintenance actions for training and operational maintenance.
The MP process begins with the maintenance concept (first step of the maintenance analysis).
The maintenance concept establishes the boundaries of the activities that follow. Areas addressed by the concept include:
  • a strategy for allocating maintenance tasks to the different levels of maintenance;
  • the repair policy regarding similar types of items contained in the subsystem/equipment;
  • the criteria for scheduling maintenance tasks;
  • the anticipated availability of resources, in gross terms, to support maintenance.
  • Preventive maintenance is first of all planned for items which need maintenance to assure their proper functionality during the complete life cycle.
For the general process of MP following points shall be considered:
  • the operational scenario must be known;
  • the results of the first analyses, that is the identification of HW needing maintenance, must be fed to the design for the incorporation of suitable set of requirements;
  • design requirements for performance of maintenance are not part of planning, they are part of maintainability; and
  • final results of identified maintenance tasks must be fed to the maintenance task analysis & logistics activities for preparation of suitable support resources.
  • In particular, the maintenance concept for an ISS P/L is something with very few degrees of freedom because the type and extent of activities feasible on-orbit is quite well defined at ISS level. At ISS level exist several technical requirements for design of equipment subject to on-orbit maintenance. On-orbit maintenance is directly related to safety considerations.
Unplanned maintenance or corrective maintenance issues are not part of a MP. Corrective needs are inserted on the bases of requests in the short term execution planning, but by definition, no long term plan can be prepared for random events.
Format Maintenance Plan
  • is an analytical process;
  • in-house format (the execution of a full MP process is not always justified);
  • see Maintenance Significant Items List (MSIL).
The MP must be translated during the operational phase into execution documents that for each planning period schedule the right maintenance tasks with the right associated resources taking into account of the operational feedbox.
