DRD-07.03 - Identification and Usage List (MIUL)
Reference: MSFC-STD-506 Materials and Process Control Standard
MSFC-SPEC-250 Protective Finishes for Space Vehicle Structures and associated Flight Equipment, General Specification for
MSFC-SPEC-522 Design Criteria for Controlling Stress Corrosion Cracking
MSFC JA-447 Mission Requirements on Facilities/Instruments/Experiments for Space Transportation System (STS) attached Payloads
MSFC-PROC-1301 Guidelines for the Implementation of required Materials Control Procedures
MSFC-HDBK-527
JSC-09604		 Materials Selection List for Space Hardware Systems
NASA-STD-6001 Flammability, Odor, Offgassing, and Compatibility Requirements and Test Procedures for Materials in Environments that Support Combustion
Purpose: To established a list of all materials to be used in the payload design. This MIUL shall be used in lieu of DML (DRD-7.02) only when ISS requirements apply.
 
Content: The MIUL shall include the following:
  • detail drawing number, title (nomenclature), and dash number,
  • next assembly number, title (nomenclature), and dash number,
  • change letter designation,
  • drawing source (contractor or vendor),
  • material and processes specification with title, revision and author identified,
  • cure number from the Materials and Process Technical Information System (MAPTIS); shall define cure conditions for non-metals only,
  • environmental data (complete environment history, including all storage, assembly and service environment, media, pressure, temperature, etc.),
  • MUA number/status (if applicable),
  • standard part number,
  • material rating/usage evaluation (based on applicable criteria of documents (see above or in the information system MAPTIS),
  • remarks (shall provide additional rationale to justify acceptance of material and processes application using MSFC-PROC-1301 for guidance.
  • The supplier shall designate on the MIUL which process specifications are critical to fabrication, control and inspection of the contract end items on the MIUL. The processes shall be submitted for review and approval of NASA;

Materials categories according MSFC-HDBK-527

Format: Example for a MIUL Instructions for completing a MIUL see below.

Usage Evaluation FLM flammability, measurements acc. to NSTS 22648
-A Meets requirements by burning less than 6 inches in the thickness and test conditions specified. This material is acc. for unlimited use.
-B Fails NASA-STD-6001 test 1 in the thickness and test conditions specified by burning at least 6 inches, but self-extinguishing before 12 inches were burned and did not burn more than 1 inch in test 2. The material is acc. with controls.
-C Fails test 1 in the thickness and test conditions specified with a burn length of at least 12 inches and burn rate of less than 0.1 inches/second down in test 2. Material is marginal.
-X Rated material is not acceptable and requires a MUA.
-S Special configuration test. See actual test report.
-I Insufficient data.
-U Untested.
AGE Static age life, acc. to Maptis/MSFC-HDBK-527
-A Rated when no changes in properties during 10 years of static life at ambient temperature and humidity.
-B Rated materials shall meet 'A' rating requirements when specific controls are imposed (e.g. stored in a controlled environment).

Instructions for Completing a Material Identification and Usage List (con't)

Usage Evaluation (con't) SCC Stress corrosion cracking, acc. to Maptis/ MSFC-HDBK-527.
-A Acceptable (material listed in MSFC-SPEC-522, table 1).
-B Acceptable with special control (material listed in MSFC-SPEC-522, table 2).
-C Acceptability must be demonstrated (material listed in MSFC-SPEC-522, table 3).
-N Not acceptable.
-U Untested and must be tested.
-P Test passed, see remarks.
COR Corrosion resistance, acc. to Maptis/MSFC-HDBK-527.
-A Acceptable and need no coating.
-B Acceptable with coating.
-U Untested and must be tested.
-X Fails and should not be used without an approved MUA.
TVS Thermal vacuum stability, acc. to Maptis/ MSFC-HDBK-527.
-A Acceptable without special cure or bake-out.
-B Acceptable with special cure or bake-out.
-C Total Mass Loss (TML) less than 3%.
Volatile Condensable Loss (VCL) less than 1%.
-U Untested and must be tested.
-X TML more than 3%, VCL more than 1%.
-I Insufficient data.
TOX Toxity / offgassing, according to NSTS 22648.
-K Meets MAC (100 lbs.) Values without special cure.
-A Meets MAC (10 lbs.) Values without special cure.
-B Meets MAC (100 lbs.  or 10 lbs. max.) Values without special cure.
-U Untested.
-V Meets MAC (85 lbs. max.) Values. MUA required.
-X MAC Values greater than that specified in NASA-STD-6001. MUA required.
MUA Status If a material requires a MUA as result of column 10-15, Column 16 shall be marked with 'X', after approval by NASA the MUA status shall be marked with 'P' (passed).
Remarks Special notes and dates for e.g. special cure, test results, add. evaluations.
