DRD-07.01 - Request For Approval of PMP (RFA)
Reference: ECSS-Q-ST-70C Space product assurance - Materials, mechanical parts and processes
Purpose: Any critical material, part or process shall be subjected of a Request For Approval (RFA) to be submitted to next customer.
A material, part or process is declared critical when:
  • it is new or non-validated (not qualified in the case of a part) for the application in question;
  • during previous use it has raised problems which remain unsolved.
Content: The RFA shall include details of the subsequent evaluation and validation to be performed.
The format of such a RFA should be standardized throughout a project and shall as minimum include the following:
  • identification details,
  • characteristics and/or function,
  • location and environment,
  • reference of evaluation/validation/qualification programmes and reports,
  • approval status.

When filling in the RFA for requesting EEE component usage, the RFA contents must be conform to a PAD contents.

Format: Example for RFA attached sheet or use an appropriate in-house form.

For payloads using NSTS/ISS a MUA shall be prepared in lieu of RFA.

Content of the RFA
The RFA shall be completed by the supplier (parts 1 to 10, 13 and 14) and the customer (parts 11,12 and 15).
  1. Header
     The header information shall identify the document as a request for approval together with the project logo and project name, RFA reference, issue revision and date.
  2. Originator
     Originator´s name and reference.
  3. Location
     Subsystem and equipment codes.
  4. Item description
     Brief description of the item.
  5. PMP information
     The DML, DMPL or DPL item number and list reference.
  6. Items status
     Items status shall be entered for:
    • manufacturer´s name and qualification reference;
    • supplier´s name and qualification status;
    • product or material specification;
    • procurement specification;
    • process or handling specification;
    • other related process or handling specifications;
    • verification or qualification specification;
    • report on verification or qualification.
  7. Reason for RFA
     The reason for the RFA shall be entered.
  8. Application and location details
     The details of the application and exact location of the item shall be entered here.
    Thereference in the CIL shall be given.
  9. Evaluation and validation programme
     Reference and details of main tests.
  10. Subcontractor supplier approval
     Subcontractor supplier approval for the first issue of the RFA.
  11. Customer initial decision
     Customer initial decision shall be entered on first issue of the RFA providing:
    • the decision concerning the proposed material,
    • the requirement to perform tests /deviation request as necessary),
    • the decision concerning the proposed test programme.
  12. Signature
     Customer´s and final customer´s (if applicable) signature.
  13. Justification results
     Justification results obtained with reference to the supplier´s validation report andconclusion.
  14. Subcontractor supplier approval
     Subcontractor supplier approval on RFA final issue.
  15. Final approval status
     Final approval status at all the levels of the final issue of the RFA by the customer and final customer (if defined in the contract)
