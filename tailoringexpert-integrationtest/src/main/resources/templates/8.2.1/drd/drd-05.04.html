DRD-05.04 - ISS Cargo/Experiment Flight Safety Certificate
Reference: ISS_OE_906 Flight Safety Certificate
NSTS/ISS 13830 Payload Safety Review and Data Submittal Requirements
П32958-106 Technical Requirements for Foreign Partner HW to be stored and Operated on the ISS Russian Segment
Purpose: The purpose of this form is to document the compliance with the ISS and Russian payload safety requirements. The objective of the safety review process is to review the payload and operations for adequate safety implementation. The mission success and any scientific objectives of the payload are the responsibility of the PO and are beyond the scope of this document and process. All projects shall certify that the flight and ground system products are safe and in compliance with the requirements of safety relevant standards as well as any applicable project specific safety requirements. The certification process shall be completed prior to delivery to the customer.
Content: The PO must present a signed certificate of the flight payload safety compliance to the PSRP executive secretary no later than 10 days prior to the Flight Readiness Review (FRR). The phase III SDP shall contain a signed Certificate of Safety Certificate. The safety analysis results shall be documented in the SDP, which includes applicable payload HR forms (JSC Form 542B and ISS_OE_1298). Guidelines for completing the flight HR forms and preparing the SDP are found in JSC 26943. Supporting data shall be prepared where applicable and also submitted for review.
Format: ISS_OE_906 Form is provided in annex.

Flight Safety Certificate (FSC) Completion Instructions

General Instructions
  • The Flight Safety Certificate (FSC) is the tool that is used by the International Partners (IPs) to communicate to each other the safety of cargo items transported or stowed/operated in each other's vehicles and segments.
  • A FSC should reference one or more items on the applicable transportation manifest or a currently on-orbit item requiring updated operations/stowage approval.
  • If approval for multiple items is to be requested on a single FSC, they must be reasonably similar (such as all belonging to a single system) and be requesting approval for the same vehicles and mission phases.
  • For payloads/experiments, the FSC is also used to document both the HW's safe design life and operational life in addition to providing the formal safety endorsement of flight readiness to the ISSP.

Fields

  1. Certificate Number:
    A unique identifier created and serialized by the requesting-side IP. For the purposes of commonality and ease of tracking ISS_OE_906, Flight Safety Certificates, the certificate numbers will conform to the following pattern which is documented in SSP 30599 and in SSP 50146 Attachment D.
    For non-experiment cargo the format is as follows:
    • Mission Number,
    • IP,
    • Mission Phase and category (1 or 2 as determined using the ISS_OE_907),
    • sequential number,
    where:
    • Mission Number is the first flight on which the HW is delivered to ISS. Mission Number is to be given in the form of 46P, 29S, HTV3, etc.
    • TR will be used to designate certification for the transportation phase only. OP will be used to designate certification for the operation phase only. If the certificate applies to both transportation and operation on ISS, rather than identifying for example TR-OP1, the TR and OP will be omitted. Thus a number alone indicates certification for both phases. If it is desired to list different categories for transportation and operation on ISS, the categories will be separated by a dash; e.g. TR1-OP2.
    • Sequential Number is a three-digit number denoting the serial number of the FSC/ISS_OE_906 within the set of safety certificates for the given mission. There is one numerical sequence for all flight phases and cargo categories, for each mission.
    For experiment cargo the format is as follows:
    • Experiment Designator,
    • Space Agency,
    • Category,
    • Expedition,
    • Sequential number
    where
    • The Experiment Designator is the designation of the experiment within a program of experiments (e.g. for Russian experiments, MBI No. – for medical and biological experiments).
    • Space Agency is the designator of the space agency which issues the experiment for use on the ISS.
    • Category is 1 or 2, by flight phase, as determined using the ISS_OE_907 "Multilateral Category 1 Constraints" form.
    • Expedition is the number of the Expedition when the experiment is planned to be performed within the ISS program, e.g. ISS25.
    • Sequential Number is a three-digit number designating the number of the FSC/ISS_OE_906 within that specific Expedition.
    For NASA items, the Certificate Number will be generated by the appropriate NASA safety review panel POC after the FSC is submitted for approval. IP Certificate Numbers shall be provided/maintained by the respective IP safety organization
  2. Cargo Item Provider:
    Put a check mark in the box corresponding to the hardware item's owner (i.e. check the box marked "NASA").
  3. Cargo Identification:
    In the Cargo Item Name box, list part name exactly as written on the applicable flight manifest. In the corresponding Part Number box, list the part number exactly as it is written in the applicable flight manifest.

    An attachment may be used if a FSC contains more items than will fit in the allotted spaces, or as needed to provide a detailed description of the content).

    For multiple hardware items, number them sequentially so that they can be matched to their descriptions in section VIII.
    e.g.:
    1. EVA Tool, XYZ-123
    2. EVA Tool Storage Bag, ABC-789
    3. EVA Fasteners, ZYX-345
  4. Transportation Safety Requirements:
    This section identifies the vehicle(s) on which you are requesting permission to be transported or returned/disposed. It also identifies which safety process was followed to request permission, which safety requirements were complied with, and which category the HW items in question fit into.
    • Step 1: Select the vehicle for which you are requesting permission for the HW to be transported or returned/disposed.

      ESA does not require approval for items to be disposed of on ATV.

    • Step 2: Select the appropriate safety process document that is to be followed for requesting permission for the selected vehicles and segments. For example:
      Safety data exchanges through the JARSWG are in accordance with SSP 50146 Attachment D. This includes Russian cargoes and payloads on the ISS and U.S. Visiting Vehicles, and US cargoes and payloads on the Progress, Soyuz, and Russian Segment.
    • Step 3: Select the appropriate category that the HW fits into. Category 1 is defined by the form, ISS_OE_907. Category 2 consists of those items that do not meet the constraints of Category 1
    • Step 4: Select the appropriate launch requirements documents that the hardware item in question must comply with. The HW provider is REQUIRED to assess each HW item for compliance with the safety requirements of the vehicle on which the item is to be transported or returned/disposed. The HW item must meet the safety requirements of the applicable transport vehicle when subjected to that vehicle's specific environments (vibration, shock and acceleration loading, depress rates, humidity and temperature ranges, etc.).
    • Step 5: If permission to return or to be disposed of on the Soyuz is requested, specify whether only disposal, only return, or both disposal and return are being requested by checking the appropriate boxes in the header row next to the headings "Return" and "Disposal".
  5. Operations/Stowage Safety Requirements:
    This section identifies the segments in which you are requesting permission to be stowed or operated. It also identifies which safety process was followed to request permission, which safety requirements were complied with, and which category the HW items in question fit into.
    • Step 1: Select the segment for which you are requesting permission for the hardware to be stowed or operated.
    • Step 2: Select the appropriate safety process document that is to be followed for requesting permission for the selected segments. For example: Safety data exchanges through the JARSWG are in accordance with SSP 50146 Attachment D.
    • Step 3: Select the appropriate category that the hardware fits into. Category 1 is defined by the form, ISS_OE_907. Category 2 consists of those items that do not meet the constraints of Category 1.
    • Step 4: Select the appropriate Stowage/Operations requirements documents that the hardware item in question must comply with. The hardware provider is REQUIRED to assess each hardware item for compliance with the safety requirements of the segment on which the item is to be stowed or operated.
  6. Signatures:
    Cargo Item Provider Certification / Safety Panel Approval (select appropriate dropdown box in the form):
    • The project manager's or hardware owner's signature indicates that all of the data entered on the certificate is accurate and true. It is the project manager's responsibility to ensure that the hardware item meets all of the marked safety requirements.
    • A NASA SRP/PSRP chair's signature or the signature of the panel's executive officer will be obtained after internal NASA–only review, before the form, ISS_OE_906 is sent to an IP.
    • In cases where the FSC will require approval by the ISS Safety Panel in addition to the Receiving Segment Owner, the ISS Safety Panel will sign in this section. (This does not apply to NASA safety certificates; it is for IPs that need to obtain ISS safety panel approval.)
    ISS Segment Owner Concurrence:
    • Utilized by receiving-side IP
    Transport Vehicle Owner Approval:
    • Utilized by receiving-side IP
  7. Baseline Safety Documentation:
    This section is where the documents are listed to ensure the correct documents are received and reviewed by the IP. The documents to attach to the FSC will depend on the IP and the category of the hardware. Contact the appropriate safety review panel representative for further guidance. If hazard reports, non-compliance reports or entire safety data packages are required to be submitted to an IP, they will be attached to the FSC. Data deliverables for NASA and Russian cargoes are defined in SSP 50146 Attachment D. In most cases, only the hazard reports/NCR's addressing the constraints on the form, ISS_OE_ 907 that the item does not meet need to be attached.
    • If no documents are required or will be attached, leave section VII blank.
    • If there is not enough space to list all of the documents, attach a sheet with additional rows containing the same information as in this section of the certificate.
    • Document Reference No.: Enter the document number for the attached document in this field.
      ex. JSC 12345, NCR-XXX-123, UNQ-XXX-123 etc.
    • Document Title: Enter the document name in this field.
      ex. SAR for the XXX, XXX Noncompliance, XXX Hazard etc.
    • Date: Enter the date that the document gained final approval by the requestingside IP.
    • Any transmission of hazard reports and technical data shall go through the appropriate export control by the HW owner prior to the JARSWG POC forwarding the data to the IP.
  8. Cargo Item(s) Description:
    In the event that multiple items are requesting approval on a single FSC, the items should be sequentially numbered in each of the following sections so that the descriptions can be matched up with the item names and part numbers in section I.

Function/How it Works:

General executive summary-type description on what the hardware is, what it does, and how it works. The scope of this section should be to include enough detail to give someone familiar with spaceflight hardware (but unfamiliar with the system) a general understanding of what the hardware is/does. Include in this section a reference to the document number that was used to certify the item for flight (in the case of GFE items, this document number would be the GCAR)

Construction/Materials:

This section outlines what the hardware is made of and how it is constructed/put together. For each component, the materials and construction method (if applicable) should be listed.
Discuss in additional detail in this section:
  • Any materials higher than Tox 0
  • Any biological materials
  • Any liquids or gases (including alcohol or other volatiles)
  • Any non-standard materials
  • Any permanent magnets
  • Any cells/batteries (include chemistry, quantity, manufacturer/model number, etc.)

Transport Configuration:

This section should outline the configuration in which the IP will transport your HW and generically how it is to be packed into the IP's launch/return/disposal vehicle (i.e. whether it is unpackaged, in a foam-packed hard case or in a soft Nomex bag / crew transfer bag (CTB), if your hardware needs to be soft-stowed or needs to be hard-mounted in the vehicle etc.). Mark as N/A if the FSC is an operations-only certificate.
For Soyuz launches and returns, two specific statements are required to be included (reference П32928-103):
  • The HW will not create a hazard if/when the Soyuz depressurizes.
  • The HW will not create a hazard under the Soyuz acceleration and shock environments.

Stowage Configuration:

This section should outline the configuration and location in which the item will be stowed longterm in the IP's segment. Mark N/A if the FSC is not for stowage or if the item will only be temp-stowed in the IP's segment. (Temp stowage is when there is a defined time limit on the stowage in a given area and/or a different, planned, long-term location for an item that is being stowed in a given area, and the time limit or need to temp stow is driven by a specific activity/constraint in the long-term location.)

Weight, Dimensions:

State overall weights in metric units followed by the dimensions in metric units only for cargo packing purposes. If necessary, specify that the weight is for multiple cargo items together, in the form of:
XX.X kg, XX x XX x XX cm (for XX)
e.g. (for 6 items packed together): 10.7 kg, 25 x 52 x 7.8 cm (for 6)

Safe Design Life:

(Experiments only) Document the time duration or number of mission’s limits of the certification. Design life is the time period during which a payload can be retained at or restored to the specified operational condition using prescribed resources and procedures. Design life must include ground and on-orbit time, including passive stowage.

Safe Operational Life:

(Experiments only) Document the time duration or number of mission’s limits of the hardware. Operational life is defined as the time period for which the payload will perform its intended function within specified performance limits under stated conditions without any safety-related corrective maintenance, recalibration or repair. Operational life must include all ground and onorbit time. Operational life is determined by the safety-related component (e.g. seals, batteries, relief valves) with the soonest expiration date. Operational life includes the entire on-orbit interval in which the payload will be expected to remain operational, including stowage between planned operations.
The design life and operational life must be rigorously assessed, and are based on the certified life of safety-related component(s) i.e. seals, relief valves, regulators, batteries, structures. They cannot be based on arbitrary factors such as estimated mission duration. A specific date or reference document must be provided to determine when the design/operational life expires. For example, if a payload's safe operational life is determined by a seal with a specific expiration date, that date would be noted on the certificate as the operational expiration date. If a payload's safe operational life begins upon ground processing, launch or activation, then the initiating event must be referenced on the certificate (for example: One year from completion of ground processing operations on XX/XX/XXXX).
