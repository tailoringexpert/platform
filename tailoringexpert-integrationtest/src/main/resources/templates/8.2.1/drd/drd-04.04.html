DRD-04.04 - Reliability Block Diagram
Reference: MIL-HDBK-338 Electronic Reliability Design Handbook
ECSS-Q-ST-30-02C Space product assurance - Failure Modes, effects (and criticality) analysis (FMEA/FMECA)
Purpose: To show, by means of a logic diagram, the effects of failure of individual elements on the item mission success.
Content: Each reliability block diagram shall have a title, including the identification of the analysed item and of the required item mission. All mission phases and modes considered shall be indicated. Each block in the diagram shall bear the identification (e.g. connector). A numerical reference for all blocks shall be provided by using a uniform numbering system in order to provide traceability through all assembly levels. It shall be possible to identify each element or function employed in the analysed item. The redundancy type shall be clearly indicated.
If the reliability block diagram is used in support of the reliability prediction the following information shall be given additionally for each block: mission time, duty cycle, active and passive failure rate for each mission phase.
For a complex system it is often convenient to prepare several block diagrams. If it is not possible to convey all of the pertinent information by the arrangement of blocks, then appropriate notation must be provided on the diagram or in the accompanying descriptions. Types of redundancy shall be described if not obvious from the diagram. Where failure of a redundant element degrades performance or places additional stresses on the items in alternate paths, it should be so noted. In addition, items that may be repaired or replaced during a mission should be identified and monitoring intervals for those items should be stated.
Format: In-house format may be used if it contains the information included in this DRD.
