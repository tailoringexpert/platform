DRD-04.09 - Hardware Software Interaction Analysis (HSIA)
Reference: ECSS-Q-ST-30-02C Space product assurance - Failure Modes, effects (and criticality) analysis (FMEA/FMECA)
ECSS-Q-ST-40C Space product assurance - Safety
ECSS-Q-ST-80C Space product assurance - Software product assurance
Purpose: SW reaction to HW failures is addressed by the HSIA. The analysis shall verify that the SW is specified to react to HW failures as required. This shall be performed at the level of the Software Requirement Document (SRD). The HSIA may be included in the FMECA.
The HSIA can be performed with the aid of the check-list in annex.
Content: To ensure that the SW is designed to react correctly to hardware failures. The functional analysis shall take into account the interaction of the SW with its environment (system H/W, human intervention, etc.) jointly with the system level analysis.
The HSIA shall be performed concurrently with the FMEA/FMECA for all HW products involving SW (e.g. SW controlled). The analysis shall be provided early in the programme to influence the design process and the necessary system requirements (HW and SW).
The SW specialists shall manage the HW/SW interaction throughout the project by performing the following activities:
  • assessment and allocation of requirements (e.g. safety: failuretolerant);
  • define/describe HW and SW interaction design or methods;
  • define/describe HW and SW interaction operational modes;
  • identification of parameters to trigger the SW;
  • hazard events / safety related non-conformances detection, identification and evaluation according to applicable FDIR requirements for the system;
  • hazard prevention, reduction and control;
  • failure mode: summary of failure mode description
The HSIA shall be performed to provide the following results:
  • inputs to the list of critical items (CIL),
  • recommendations and remarks
Format: The HSIA shall be documented by completing the attached forms
SW that implements or controls safety-critical functions shall be subject to safety analysis (ECSS-Q-ST-40C).
The HW and SW configuration shall be identified and documented as part of the test documentation.
The FMEA/FMECA worksheet shall be completed as follows:
  • in each column: add "S/W" if SW is involved,
  • local/end effect: add point 10 of HSIA form,
  • failure detection: add points 6 and 7 of HSIA form,
  • recovery/compensation: add points 8 and 9 of HSIA form
