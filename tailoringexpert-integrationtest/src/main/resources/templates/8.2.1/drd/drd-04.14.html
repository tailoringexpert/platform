DRD-04.14 - Zonal analysis (ZA)
Reference: ECSS-Q-ST-30C Space Product Assurance - Dependability
ECSS-Q-ST-40C Space Product Assurance - Safety
SAE ARP 4754 Certification Considerations for Highly-Integrated or Complex Aircraft Systems
SAE ARP 4761 Guidelines and Methods for Conducting the Safety Assessment Process an Civil Airborne Systems and Equipment is a Standard from SAE
Purpose: Safety critical systems are designed and analysed carefully in order to verify not only that they operate as they were intended, but also to prevent them from operating in any way that is not desired (e.g. unanticipated component and subsystem interactions).
Zonal (Safety) Analysis is the:
  • systematic inspection of the geographical locations of the components and interconnections of a system,
  • evaluation of potential system-to-system interactions with and without failures, and
  • assessment of the severity of potential hazards inherent in the system installation.
Zonal Analysis should be performed where redundancy is used to reduce the probability of losing a critical function, because the consequence of a single undesirable event (e.g. inadvertently actuating a safety critical function) eliminating all of a system's redundancy is sometimes quite severe.
A Zonal Analysis should examine each physical zone of the spacecraft to ensure that equipment installation and potential physical interference with adjacent systems do not violate the independence requirements of the systems.
Content: The Zonal Analysis may be performed at any stage of the design process. The analysis has potential influence on system architecture. Note that not all hazards are the result of faults. Hazards can also be caused by unanticipated sequences of interactions between components or subsystems.

The objective of the Zonal Analysis shall ensure that equipment installation meets the adequate safety requirements regarding:
  1. basic installation rules and space practices;
  2. interaction between subsystems;
  3. implication of human errors;
  4. effects of external events.
Zonal Analysis is a technique used in hazard identification to identify the interactions between components or subsystems, and the undesired consequences of those interactions (flow/interactions between parts of a system).

Conventional Zonal Analysis splits the system up into various physical zones and considers how the behavior in one zone (both under normal operation and under failure) may affect the integrity and behavior in other zones. Other forms of analysis may be applied to the individual components; Zonal Analysis considers their interactions.

Zonal Analysis is usually performed with physical or digital mock-up support and prototypes. It can also be carried out by means of an audit of the design and a checklist appropriate to the domain. Zonal Analysis takes various forms and seems poorly documented in comparison with other techniques.

The value of performing a comprehensive Zonal Analysis is often not immediately obvious.
