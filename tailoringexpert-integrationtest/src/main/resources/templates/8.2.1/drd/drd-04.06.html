DRD-04.06 - Functional Block Diagram
Reference: ECSS-Q-ST-30-02C Space product assurance - Failure Modes, effects (and criticality) analysis (FMEA/FMECA)
Purpose: In order to design, develop and prove a system/subsystem, the mission and consequent functions, that the system shall perform, shall be clearly established. A functional block diagram shall be constructed to depict the elements of the analysed systems and their functional interdependencies.
Content: A functional block diagram shall be a system representation which emphasizes interfaces and interrelationships between functional entities (input/output flows). The elements of the analysed product are shown as blocks. A numerical reference for all blocks in the functional block diagram shall be provided by using a standard numbering system to provide consistent identification of items and functions. The functional interdependencies between the blocks are indicated on the connection lines between the blocks. The following functional analysis steps shall be implemented:
  • define the system and boundaries of the system under analysis by using the relevant requirements documents;
  • define the level of detail (depth of analysis);
  • identify the system functions/operational modes/operational scenarios;
  • represent the system by preparing the function tree(s), or functional matrix(ces), or functional block diagram(s).
Format: An example of FBD is provided hereafter. The system functionality shall be distributed throughout the different design levels (e.g. systems, subsystems, units).
