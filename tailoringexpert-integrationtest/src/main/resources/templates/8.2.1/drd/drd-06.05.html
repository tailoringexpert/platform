DRD-06.05 - EEE-Radiation Control Plan
Reference: ESA-PSS-01-609 Radiation Design Handbook
Goddard Space Flight Center Preferred Parts List- PPL (Radiation Effects and Goddard Total Dose Radiation Test Data)
MIL-STD-750D Test Method Standard for Semiconductor Devices
MIL-STD-883 Test Methods and Procedures for Microelectronics
MIL-HDBK-279 Total Dose Hardness Assurance Guidelines for Semiconductor Devices and Microcircuits
ECSS-Q-ST-60C Space Product Assurance - EEE Components
Purpose: A radiation risk assessment for any electronic device shall be performed and shall include the determination of total dose damage and Single Event Effect (SEE) susceptibility of the device caused by the projected radiation environment of the spacecraft. It should be noted that, while the total dose radiation on a device can vary significantly with the amount of shielding interposed between the device and the outside environment, the heavy ion and high-energy proton fluencies (and hence the SEE susceptibility) do not change significantly with shielding. SEEs are caused by a high energy single ion (heavy ion or energetic proton) passing through a device.
SEEs include:
  • Single Event Upsets (SEU),
  • Single Event Latchup (SEL),
  • Single Event Induced Burnout (SEB),
  • Single Event Gate Rupture (SEGR).
While SEUs are non-destructive, and do not cause permanent damage to the device, the other single event effects can be destructive under certain conditions.
Content: The total dose and SEE hardness assurance requiring the following major steps:
  • the identification of radiation sensitive part types;
  • the acquisition of radiation characterization data for each critical parameter of radiation sensitive part types fabricated by the same manufacture;
  • a worst case circuit analysis to determine the maximum parameter degradation of each critical device parameter that the circuit application will tolerate;
  • the presentation of a simple form (Radiation Control Plan) for recording the protective action taken, for each sensitive part in the system or subsystem.
This form shall identify the circuit components, the manufacturer, the critical parameters and their worst case design values, the maximum radiation level that the device will be subjected to and the worst case value of the parameter after radiation.
