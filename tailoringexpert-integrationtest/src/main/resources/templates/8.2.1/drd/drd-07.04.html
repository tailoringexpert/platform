DRD-07.04 - Materials Usage Agreement (MUA)
Reference: MSFC-STD-506 Materials and Process Control Standard
MSFC-SPEC-250 Protective Finishes for Space Vehicle Structures and associated Flight Equipment, General Specification for
MSFC-SPEC-522 Design Criteria for Controlling Stress Corrosion Cracking
MSFC JA-447 Mission Requirements on Facilities/Instruments/Experiments for Space Transportation System (STS) attached Payloads
MSFC-PROC-1301 Guidelines for the Implementation of required Materials Control Procedures
MSFC-HDBK-527
JSC-09604		 Materials Selection List for Space Hardware Systems
NASA-STD-6001 Flammability, Odor, Offgassing, and Compatibility Requirements and Test Procedures for Materials in Environments that Support Combustion
JSC-SPEC-SP-R-0022A Vacuum Stability Requirements Of Polymeric Materials For Spacecraft Applications
Purpose: To request the use of materials and processes which do not have an "A" rating per MSFC-HDBK-527 for its planned application. This MUA shall be used in lieu of RFA only when ISS requirements apply.
Content: The MUA shall contain sufficient information with supporting detailed rationale to permit the use of non "A" rated material and processes. Topics which might be covered under rationale are:
  • Corrosion
    Passivation, surface treatment, overcoated, etc.
  • Stress Corrosion Cracking
    Evaluation of sustained tensile stress (see stress evaluation form MSFC-SPEC-522).
  • Flammability
    Thickness, quantity, closed container, ignition source, media for ground & flight.
  • Toxicity/Offgassing/Odor
    Thickness, quantity, closed container, media, overcoated, encapsulated.
  • Thermal Vacuum Stability
    Quantity, exposed surface area, proximity to sensitive surfaces, overcoated, encapsulated.

The MUA is evaluated by MSFC and approved/disapproved by the Materials Application Evaluation Board (MAEB). If approved, it is valid for the specific material, in the specific usage for that HW.

Format: Example for a MUA (MSFC document JA-447). Description and guidelines for preparation of MUA see below.

Rationale The rationale should address the following areas and do not try to be too brief. Write the rationale such that an engineer not familiar with your programme can understand your specific programme usage. Cost, previous usage, schedule are not reasons for use of a material.
a) Basic Ground Rules

1. Materials in a sealed container do require MUA`s. Rationale should be as a group of all materials in container.
2. Materials listed as acceptable in MSFC-HDBK-527 do not normally require MUA`s.
3. Materials listed in MSFC-HDBK-527 that are rated acceptable in other ways need only address the ways that are rated unacceptable if they are used in contact with the media/fluid specified.
Note: If a material is rated "X" for TVS and "A" for TOX, if the material is used in the Crew Bay; TVS need not be addressed. Similarly, if a material is rated "X" for hydrazine but is not used in hydrazine, do not address the "X" rating.
b) Areas to be addressed - Materials are divided into basic categories, metallic and non-metallic.

MSFC-HDBK-527 describes how materials are rated for each requirement/fluid. Each area should be addressed in the MUA for the non A-rated area, if the material application "sees" the media/requirement. Acceptable factors need not be addressed. Disciplines to be addressed for metals are stress corrosion, stress corrosion cracking, fluid system compatibility, flammability, dissimilar metals.
Stress Corrosion
Stress Corrosion Cracking Materials susceptible to stress corrosion cracking require evaluation of sustained tensile stresses. The stress evaluation form must be completed for this evaluation.
Fluid Systems Compatibility If the material application is not used in a fluid system, MUA is not required.
Flammability If the metal is not flammable, MUA is not required. If the metal is flammable in the thickness used, you must address these questions: Is the metal in a closed container? Is there an ignition source in the proximity? What is the media for ground ops and flight?
Factors to be addressed for non metallic materials are: flammability, toxicity/offgassing, thermal vacuum stability, age life, fluid systems compatibility.
Flammability Items to be addressed here are:

quantity, is the box closed or open, what is the media for ground test and flight and is there an ignition source in the proximity.
Toxicity/Offgassing (for items in habitable environment, crew bay only). Items to be addressed are: Quantity, is the material coated to prevent outgassing? Will the material be degraded by outgassing? Will the outgassing constituent be condensed on surrounding equipment and be detrimental? Is the box closed or open?
Age Life (see definition in MSFC-HDBK-527) The key here is that you must verify for yourself that the material will last for the duration of the mission. This will include shelf life, fabrication, testing, storage and mission life.
Fluid System Compatibility If the material is not in contact with degrading corrosive fluids, a MUA is not required. However, if the materials are used in contact with fluids that are not compatible, justification must be supplied.
