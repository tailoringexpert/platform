DRD-03.10 - End Item Data Package (EIDP) for deliverable Hardware (ESA)
Reference: ECSS-Q-ST-20C Space Product Assurance - Quality assurance
ECSS-E-ST-10-02C Space Engineering - Verification
Purpose: The EIDP is the collection of the data related to the manufacturing, assembly, integration and test of a deliverable configuration item which provides the necessary traceability and events record.
The EIDP shall constitute the basis to support the formal acceptance of the product.
The document shall be built from the beginning of the activity for all relevant verification levels (i.e. MIP, TRR or TRB) as required by the contractual requirements. It is used to perform the TRB or DRB with the customer during the acceptance review of deliverable HW.
Content:

Title (see example cover page)

Proper references shall be selected to clearly identify the product and the relevant applicable documents.
For example:
  • Item description
  • Product specification
  • Serial number
  • Drawing
  • Model
  • CI number
  • Contract number.

Section 1

  • The customer follow-up sheet to record all the events after the product final delivery
  • The customer acceptance certificate
  • The DRB minutes

    The shipping document even if it is not part of the EIDP shall be available at the time of delivery.

Section 2

The EIDP front sheet and the contents.

Section 3

The EIDP change record.

Section 4

The product certificate of conformity as defined in the relevant DRD, including NCR list.

Section 5

As-design as-built configuration status.

Section 6

The summary and status of RFDs and RFWs raised and processed on the product.

Section 7

Product definition documents necessary for further integration, testing and operation in higher level assemblies including the SW used to operate the item and the product user or operating manuals.

Section 8

The product logbook, which includes all the data recorded in chronological order to allow a full traceability of the manufacturing, assembly, integration and test activities performed on the item.

Section 9

The procedures necessary for the proper handling of the product after its final delivery. Typically these procedures cover aspects, such as:
  • packing,
  • handling,
  • storage,
  • transportation,
  • safety, and
  • cleanliness.

Section 10

The copies of the product test reports, or as a minimum the list of the documents with the identification of their location.

Section 11

The list of all the ground support equipment (MGSE, EGSE, FGSE, OGSE) with the reference to the relevant EIDPs and SW product.

Section 12

The list of EIDPs or logbooks of units and subsystem supplied by lower tier suppliers.

Section 13

The list of the loose items and not installed items supplied with the product.

Section 14

This section shall be identified as "Other data and remarks" and shall be used to add any useful information or data relevant to the product and not included in the standard EIDP sections.
Format: Supplier in-house document (structure use following list, the completeness is to be checked against the list).
