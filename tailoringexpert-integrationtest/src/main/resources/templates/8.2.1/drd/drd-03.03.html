DRD-03.03 - Request for Deviation (RfD)
Reference: ECSS-Q-ST-20C Space product assurance - Quality assurance
ECSS-Q-ST-10-09C Space Product Assurance - Nonconformance control system
ECSS-M-ST-40C Space project management - Configuration and information management
Purpose: The objective of the request for deviation is to be the vehicle for requiring and agreeing the departure from a customer's requirement that is part of an approved configuration baseline.
Content: Regardless of the form used, each RFD shall contain as a minimum the following information:
  • designation as a RFD or RFW,
  • project identification,
  • RFD number,
  • model designation,
  • controlled documents affected (number, issue, title),
  • effect on cost/price
    (if a reduction in contract cost or price is being offered as a concession for the proposed authorized requirement deviation, the proposed reduction shall be stated. If the RFD would result in a contract cost/price increase or major milestone impact, the deviation request shall be attached to an ECP/CCN and processed accordingly),
  • effect on delivery schedule,
  • effect on quantity,
  • effect on interface, performance
    (if the RFD has an impact on I/F and/or performance, describe such effects in an enclosure and reference the enclosure at appropriate point
  • description
    (describe in detail the nature of the proposed deviation from the technical requirements. The RFD shall be analyzed in depth to determine whether and how it affects the product performance, assurance, dependability, safety, operation and logistics. Marked up drawings shall be attached, when necessary),
  • justification/reason
    (explain the reason which makes it impossible or unreasonable to comply with the technical requirements within the specified delivery schedule. Also state why a RFD is proposed in lieu of a permanent design change. Justification requirements concerning waived EEE-Parts see next page),
  • originator (company, name, signature),
  • approval need date (date by which approval is required to minimize cost and/or schedule impact).
Format: The in-house format to be used shall be similar to the format below and shall contain at least the above mentioned content.

Justification requirements concerning waived EEE-Parts:

At least the following justification should be provided:
  1. Data should be provided sufficiently detailing why the proposed part is needed including advantages and disadvantages (technical, availability, effect on cost/price, effect on delivery schedule).
  2. Provision of data that substantiates that the electrical performance characterizations are compatible with the life reliability assurance criteria and the application.
    For example:
    • When a part is proposed for an application beyond the recommended device parameters criteria supplemental justification should be given.
    • For example, if a tantalum capacitor is intended for use at a frequency not recommended by the typical application, objective data should be presented to show that the capacitor will perform the desired circuit function without degradation of reliability over the expected life cycle mission of the system or equipment.
    • When any part is proposed for an application in which circuit performance depends on a characteristic or parameter that is not controlled by the component specification, justification for use of the part shall include substantive data to show that the parameter will be maintained.
    • Such justification should include data that shows that interrelated parameters are controlled to the extent necessary to assure that the critical parameter will be consistently within established limits or that supplementary parameter limits have not been imposed by specification which add prohibitive cost or effect availability.
  3. Therefore - Copy of relevant data sheet should be provided if parameters of proposed device are different to required controlled device parameters.
  4. Describe in detail whether and how it affects the product performance, PA, dependability, safety, operation and logistics.
  5. Effected quantity.
  6. Part relevant in-house screening programme (copy from data book if available).
